PreSquared Data Science:
Why our Algorithms Stand Out


PreSquared’s predictive algorithms are created using state of the art predictive modeling approaches, trained on hundreds of millions of health records, representing a diverse sample across the healthcare industry.

Our data sources span both Medicare and Medicaid populations and include inpatient and outpatient hospital records, primary care and specialist claims, and information from pharmacy and laboratory encounters. We also include a wealth of data on social determinants of health, including micro-level environmental risk factors that affect the health and wellbeing of the population.

Our models are constantly adapting to changes in the healthcare landscape.


In all, we have over 200 risk factors that our discrete-time survival models can choose from, which allows the models to re-rank each reason for risk according to the changing healthcare landscape.

This has been extremely important in ensuring that our algorithms are statistically robust even in the face of dramatic utilization shocks, such as the one driven by the COVID-19 pandemic. Our scores, and the risk factors driving those scores, were able to keep pace with the utilization shifts that occurred during each phase of the pandemic, and allowed providers to keep prioritizing their riskiest patients even through the worst surges.
